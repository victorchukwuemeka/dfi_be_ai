# Week 6: Synthesis and Insight — Turning Messy Notes into Decisions

## What is Synthesis?
Synthesis is combining multiple pieces of information to produce a coherent understanding — not just summarizing, but finding connections, contradictions, and patterns.

## The Synthesis Workflow

### Step 1: Gather
Collect all source material: notes, transcripts, emails, data, reports.

### Step 2: Extract
For each source, extract:
- Key facts (objective)
- Key opinions (subjective)
- Open questions

### Step 3: Identify Patterns
Prompt pattern:
```
I have gathered [N] sources on [topic]. For each source I've extracted key facts and opinions.
Synthesize these into:
1. Points of agreement (across 3+ sources)
2. Points of disagreement or tension
3. Gaps (important questions not addressed)
4. Recommended next steps

Sources:
[source 1]
[source 2]
...
```

### Step 4: Decide
End with a decision or recommendation. Synthesis without a decision is just a longer document.

## Prompt Patterns for Synthesis

### Meeting Notes to Action Items
```
Role: Facilitator.
Task: Convert these meeting notes into decisions and action items.
Output format:
- Decisions made:
- Action items (owner, due date):
- Items deferred:
```

### Research Synthesis
```
Role: Research analyst.
Task: Synthesize these [N] articles on [topic].
Identify:
- Consensus findings
- Competing views
- Confidence level for each finding (high/medium/low based on source quality and quantity)
```

### Decision Matrix
```
Given these options and criteria, create a decision matrix:
Options: [A, B, C]
Criteria: [cost, time, impact, risk] (each weighted 1-5)
Rate each option and recommend one.
```
